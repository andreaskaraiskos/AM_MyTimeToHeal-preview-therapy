MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT AND CRISIS INFORMATION

It often takes a great deal of courage to make that initial contact with a therapist. It’s a big decision to make and often one that you have considered for some time. However, there are moments when there is a sense of great urgency and you may not know where to turn to for that ‘crisis’ moment. It may be that you are in therapy, and you are unable to contact your counsellor because it is perhaps out of hours, they are away or unavailable. You may be struggling financially and cannot afford to access a private therapist. There may be a multitude of reasons why you may feel you need some mental health support.
A Mental Health Crisis can be equally as concerning as a physical emergency, you may feel an urgency to reach out for help and so below are a list of numbers that may be of some use to you in times such as these.
In the first instance, always call your GP or Health Professional and if you feel able to do so, contact someone you trust and let them know what you are experiencing. 
In the event where the GP isn’t able to immediately assist you or offer you an appointment, then try one of the following options* by either clicking on the link or calling the number.
If you are worried about your immediate safety or have thoughts about hurting yourself or other people call 999 for the emergency services or go to your nearest A & E

Mental Health Crisis Lines

If you are feeling distressed and need to speak with someone, the agencies below may be able to offer some immediate advice or support:

  • NHS Direct on 111
  • Central & North West London Mental Health Crisis Line on 0800 0234 650
  • West London Mental Health Crisis Line on 0300 1234 244
  • Samaritans (Not just for suicide) on 116 12 or Email jo@samaritans.org
  • Crisis Text Line UK (currently in its pilot stage) - 85258

Mental Health Support or Counselling Services

  • CALM (Campaign for Living Miserably) – 0800 58 58 58 
  • Papyrus – (for under 35s & prevention of suicide) – 0800 068 41 41 or Text 07786 209 697 or Email pat@papyrus-uk.org
  • The Silver Line – (A confidential free helpline for older people in the UK open 24 hours a day) – 0800 4 70 80 90
  • Childline – (for under 19s – whatever your worry) – 0800 1111
  • Cruse Bereavement Care - 0808 808 167
  • Kooth (Online support for young people)
  • Mind (Hillingdon) or call 01895 271559
  • Mind (Hammersmith and Fulham) or call enquiries@headstrongcounselling.co.uk
